# Case Brief: State of Maharashtra v. Aryan Mehta (Case No. 2024/CR/891)

## 1. Case Overview
**Jurisdiction:** Sessions Court, Mumbai, Maharashtra
**Charges:** 
- Section 103 of Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) / Sec 302 IPC (Murder)
- Section 238 of BNS / Sec 201 IPC (Causing disappearance of evidence)
- Section 66F of Information Technology Act (Cyber terrorism/tampering)

**Accused (Defendant):** Aryan Mehta (32), Chief Technology Officer at 'InnovaTech Solutions'.
**Victim:** Ravi Sharma (45), CEO and majority shareholder of 'InnovaTech Solutions'.

---

## 2. Facts of the Case
On the night of October 14th, 2024, between 11:00 PM and 1:00 AM, Ravi Sharma was found dead in his private office due to blunt force trauma. There were no signs of forced entry. 

Earlier that week, Ravi had initiated legal proceedings to oust Aryan from the company due to disputes over a new AI product launch. This provides a clear motive.

Aryan Mehta claims he was at his apartment, 15 kilometers away, the entire night. To prove this, he provided his "Smart Home" network logs, which show his biometric door lock engaging at 9:00 PM and not opening again until 8:00 AM the next day. Furthermore, the logs show his smart TV was streaming a movie from 11:15 PM to 1:30 AM.

---

## 3. The Prosecution's Case (The "Phantom Override" Theory)
The Prosecution argues that the smart home logs are a fabricated alibi. 

1. **The Hack:** Aryan, being a cybersecurity expert, allegedly wrote a script to spoof his smart home router, simulating biometric presence and network activity (the movie streaming) while he sneaked out through a ground-floor window.
2. **The CCTV:** A grainy CCTV camera on the street outside the victim's office captured a hooded figure matching Aryan's exact height and build entering the premises at 11:45 PM.
3. **Digital Footprint:** Cyber forensics recovered a deleted search history from Aryan's secondary laptop detailing "how to spoof IoT router packets to bypass biometric logs."

---

## 4. The Defense's Case (The Ironclad Digital Alibi)
The Defense maintains Aryan is entirely innocent and is being framed.

1. **Digital Alibi:** The Smart Home logs are encrypted and stored on a third-party cloud server (SecureHome Inc.). The server company provided an affidavit stating there was no unauthorized breach or spoofing detected on their end.
2. **Circumstantial Video:** The CCTV footage is extremely poor quality. It is raining, the figure is wearing a hood, and facial recognition algorithms completely failed to match the figure to Aryan.
3. **Alternate Suspect:** The Defense points out that Ravi Sharma had recently fired three senior executives who also had access to the building, none of whom were thoroughly investigated.

---

## 5. Key Evidence Submitted (Exhibits)
- **Exhibit A (Autopsy Report):** Confirms time of death between 11:30 PM and 12:30 AM.
- **Exhibit B (SecureHome Cloud Logs):** Shows Aryan's house door remained locked and network was actively streaming data during the time of the murder.
- **Exhibit C (Forensic Laptop Image):** Shows deleted internet searches related to IoT hacking, though Defense claims this was "research for InnovaTech's new cybersecurity product."
- **Exhibit D (CCTV Snapshot):** A blurry image of a suspect entering the victim's building at 11:45 PM.

---

## 6. Trial Objective
The court must determine if the digital alibi is genuine or a sophisticated fabrication, and whether the circumstantial evidence (motive + deleted searches + grainy CCTV) is enough to prove Aryan Mehta's guilt beyond a reasonable doubt.
